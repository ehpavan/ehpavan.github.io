About Me

Hi, I'm Pavan — a 16-year-old cybersecurity enthusiast from India. My journey into the world of hacking began at the age of 10, driven by curiosity and a desire to understand how systems behave under real-world conditions.

By 13, I was deeply exploring programming, Linux, networking, and offensive security. Over time, I developed hands-on skills in Python, Bash, SQL, Red Teaming, API Security, Web Security, Linux, Mobile Security, and Active Directory.

I actively participate in bug bounty programs, where I discover and responsibly report vulnerabilities, and I build automation to improve reconnaissance and vulnerability discovery efficiency.

I am currently building ZeroClyne, a platform focused on continuous security, attack surface visibility, and protecting modern web, cloud, and API-driven systems.

My long-term goal is to become a next-generation security engineer — combining deep technical research, automation, and AI to help make the internet safer.
